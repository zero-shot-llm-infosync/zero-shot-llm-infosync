A college applicant can use a TOEFL score from an exam taken within the previous year.

The TOEFL exam is required by most Ivy League colleges and universities in the United States.

The math portion of the TOEFL exam accounts for 25% of the exam's total score.

The standardized Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) is a globally recognized method of establishing language proficiency.

The United Nations requires that job applicants pass the TOEFL.

The TOEFL is only offered in Eastern European and Southeast Asian countries.

TOEFL was started in the 1960s.

TOEFL can be taken from every continent.

TOEFL has a negative score measure.
