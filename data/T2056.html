Life expectancy among patients afflicted with AIDS greatly increases with antiretroviral therapy treatment.

Most new cases of AIDS in the United States occur as a result of exposure to breast milk.

Antiretroviral therapies are capable of curing AIDS in a small percentage of patients.

Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) is no longer considered the automatic death sentence that it was in the 1980s.

Unprotected sex is the primary method of HIV transmission.
