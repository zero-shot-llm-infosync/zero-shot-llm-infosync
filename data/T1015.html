Bosnia and Herzegovina is a European country with a representative government.

Bosnia and Herzegovina was home to many bloody battles in the 20th century.

Bosnia and Herzegovina is located next to the Black sea.

Despite having several national languages, Bosnia and Herzegovina does not have a single national language.

Valentin Inzko is the longest serving high representative in the relatively short history of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

In Bosnia and Herzegovina, the president is elected to a six year term.

Sarajevo is the capital of a country with three national languages.

Most Bosnians speak Bosnian.

The legislature of Bosnia and Herzegovina has one house.
