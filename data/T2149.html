Law School Admission Test is a standardized test used for admission into Law schools in many English-speaking countries, primarily the United States.

Law School Admission Test can also be used to admission to some graduate degree programs not just law schools.

Law School Admission Test is a multi-day exam given in multiple-languages.

The Law School Admission Test has existed for over 70 years.

The writing section of the LSAT isn't considered in law school admissions.

LSAT scores from tests taken in 2006 are valid today.

The LSAT is available to take worldwide.

The LSAT's results are good for a lifetime.

The LSAT has a negative score measure.
