The PCAT exam is limited to just under three and a half hours in length.

A score of 400 is considered to be a passing grade on the PCAT exam.

PCAT scores are used by many medical school admissions boards throughout the United States and Canada.

The PCAT first was administered in the 1970s.

The PCAT's fee has stayed the same throughout its history.

The PCAT has a negative score measure.

The PCAT is used for admission purposes for students going to pharmacy colleges.

The fee for the test is cheaper for Canadian students.

The PCAT was introduced in 1970.
